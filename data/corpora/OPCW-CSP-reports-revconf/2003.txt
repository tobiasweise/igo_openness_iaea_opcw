REPORT OF THE EIGHTH SESSION 
OF THE CONFERENCE OF THE STATES PARTIES 
20  24 OCTOBER 2003 


1. 
AGENDA ITEM ONE  Opening of the session 
1.1 
The Eighth Session of the Conference of the States Parties (hereinafter the 
Conference) was opened at 11:02 on 20 October 2003 by the Chairman of the 
Seventh Session of the Conference, Ambassador Noureddine Djoudi of Algeria. The 
Conference received a message from the Secretary-General of the United Nations 
(C-8/6, dated 20 October 2003). 
1.2 
The following 117 States Parties participated in the Eighth Session of the Conference: 
Albania, Algeria, Andorra, Argentina, Armenia, Australia, Austria, Azerbaijan, 
Bangladesh, Belarus, Belgium, Bolivia, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Brazil, Bulgaria, 
Burkina Faso, Burundi, Cameroon, Canada, Chile, China, Colombia, Costa Rica, 
Côte dIvoire, Croatia, Cuba, Cyprus, Czech Republic, Denmark, Ecuador, 
El Salvador, Eritrea, Estonia, Ethiopia, Finland, France, Gabon, Gambia, Georgia, 
Germany, Ghana, Greece, Guatemala, Holy See, Hungary, Iceland, India, Indonesia, 
Iran (Islamic Republic of), Ireland, Italy, Jamaica, Japan, Jordan, Kazakhstan, Kenya, 
Kuwait, Latvia, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malaysia, Malta, Mauritius, Mexico, 
Monaco, Mongolia, Morocco, Namibia, Nepal, Netherlands, New Zealand, 
Nicaragua, Nigeria, Norway, Oman, Pakistan, Panama, Paraguay, Peru, Philippines, 
Poland, Portugal, Qatar, Republic of Korea, Republic of Moldova, Romania, Russian 
Federation, Saudi Arabia, Senegal, Serbia and Montenegro, Singapore, Slovakia, 
Slovenia, South Africa, Spain, Sri Lanka, Sudan, Swaziland, Sweden, Switzerland, 
Tajikistan, Thailand, The former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia, Tonga, Trinidad 
and Tobago, Tunisia, Turkey, Ukraine, United Arab Emirates, United Kingdom of 
Great Britain and Northern Ireland, United States of America, Uruguay, Uzbekistan, 
Venezuela, Viet Nam, Yemen, and Zambia. 
1.3 
In accordance with Rule 67 of its Rules of Procedure, the Conference permitted the 
Republic of Burundi to exert its right to vote during this session of the Conference 
(C-8/DEC.6, dated 23 October 2003). 
1.4 
In accordance with Rule 29 of the Rules of Procedure of the Conference, the 
following signatory States participated in this session of the Conference as observers: 
Chad and Israel.

2. 
AGENDA ITEM TWO - Election of the Chairman 
In accordance with Rules 34 and 35 of its Rules of Procedure, the Conference, by 
acclamation, elected as its Chairperson Ambassador Noor Farida Ariffin of Malaysia, 
who shall hold office until her successor is elected at the next regular session of the 
Conference. 

3. 
AGENDA ITEM THREE - Election of Vice-Chairmen and other officers 
3.1 
In accordance with Rules 34 and 35 of its Rules of Procedure of, the Conference 
elected representatives of the following ten States Parties as Vice-Chairmen of the 
Conference, to hold office until their successors are elected at its next regular session: 
Bosnia and Herzegovina, Guatemala, India, Japan, Mexico, Nigeria, Poland, 
South Africa, Switzerland, and United States of America. 
3.2 
Also in accordance with Rules 34 and 35 of the same Rules, the Conference elected 
Ambassador Noureddine Djoudi of Algeria Chairman of the Committee of the Whole, 
to hold office until a new Chairman is elected at the next regular session of the 
Conference.

5. 
AGENDA ITEM FIVE - Organisation of work and establishment of subsidiary 
bodies 
5.1 
The Conference adopted appropriate recommendations of the General Committee, 
reported to it in accordance with Rule 43 of the Rules of Procedure of the Conference. 
5.2 
The Conference adopted the recommendation of the General Committee to close the 
Eighth Session of the Conference on 24 October 2003. 
6. 
AGENDA ITEM SIX - Appointment of the Credentials Committee 
In accordance with Rule 27 of the Rules of Procedure, the Conference, following the 
proposal of its Chairperson, appointed the following ten members of the Credentials 
Committee to hold office until new members are appointed at the next regular session 
of the Conference: Austria, Cameroon, Cuba, Czech Republic, Jordan, Namibia, 
Pakistan, Portugal, Ukraine, and Uruguay. 

7. 
AGENDA ITEM SEVEN - Statement by the Director-General 
The Conference noted the opening statement by the Director-General (C-8/DG.7, 
dated 20 October 2003). 

8. 
AGENDA ITEM EIGHT - General debate 
The following delegations made statements during the general debate: Italy (on behalf 
of the European Union and acceding and associated countries, and of the European 
Free Trade Association countries Iceland and Norway), the Russian Federation, 
South Africa, Singapore, Norway, China, the United States of America, Panama, 
Switzerland, Japan, Korea, the Philippines, Mexico, Malaysia (on behalf of the 
Non-Aligned Movement and China), India, the Czech Republic, Morocco, Brazil, 
France, Nigeria, Algeria, Romania, Turkey, Cuba, Ukraine, Bangladesh, Pakistan, the 
Islamic Republic of Iran, Saudi Arabia, Albania, Slovakia, Qatar, the Sudan, Canada, 
Mauritius, Viet Nam, Peru, Argentina, and Bosnia and Herzegovina.

AGENDA ITEM TEN - Report of the OPCW for 2002 
The Conference considered and approved the report of the OPCW on the 
implementation of the Chemical Weapons Convention in 2002 (C-8/5, dated

11. 
AGENDA ITEM ELEVEN - Report of the Executive Council on the 
performance of its activities 
11.1 
The Conference noted the report of the Executive Council (hereinafter the Council) 
on the performance of its activities in the period from 17 July 2002 to 27 June 2003 
(EC-34/3 C-8/3, dated 23 September 2003). The report was introduced by the 
Chairman of the Council, Ambassador Petr Kubernát of the Czech Republic, who also 
introduced the recommendations of the Council that required the attention of the 
Conference, including those made after the cut-off date for the above report. 
Recommendations of the Council to the Conference 

Extension of deadlines for the destruction of Category 1 chemical weapons stockpiles 

11.2 
In accordance with the recommendation of the Council at its Twenty-Third Meeting, 
the Conference considered and adopted a decision on a request by the Russian 
Federation for an extension of the intermediate and final deadlines for the destruction 
of its Category 1 chemical weapons (C-8/DEC.13, dated 24 October 2003). 
11.3 
In accordance with the recommendation of the Council at its Twenty-Third Meeting, 
the Conference considered and adopted a decision on a request by a State Party for 
an extension of the intermediate deadline for the destruction of its Category 1 
chemical weapons stockpiles (C-8/DEC.14, dated 24 October 2003). 
11.4 
In accordance with the recommendation of the Council at its Twenty-Third Meeting, 
the Conference considered and adopted a decision on a request by the United States 
of America for an extension of the intermediate and final deadlines for the destruction 
of its Category 1 chemical weapons (C-8/DEC.15, dated 24 October 2003). 
Procedures for revising the technical specifications for approved equipment 

11.5 
The Council at its Thirty-First Session approved, for provisional application, 
procedures for revising the technical specifications for approved equipment 
(EC-31/DEC.8, dated 12 December 2002). In accordance with the recommendation

Report of the Office of Internal Oversight for 2002 

11.9 
The Conference noted the report of the Office of Internal Oversight (OIO) for the 
period from 1 January to 31 December 2002, and the accompanying Note by the 
Director-General (EC-34/DG.4, dated 17 June 2003; Corr.1, dated 18 August 2003;

Council at its Thirty-Fourth Session. The Conference also noted the comments of the 
Council at its Thirty-Fourth Session on the work carried out by the OIO (paragraph 16 
of EC-34/5).

22 October 2003).

Plan of action regarding the implementation of Article VII obligations 

11.12 
Based on the recommendation of the Council at its Twenty-Third Meeting, and 
following a request by the First Special Session of the Conference of the States 
Parties to Review the Operation of the Chemical Weapons Convention, the 
Conference approved a plan of action regarding the implementation of Article VII 
obligations, with the objective of fostering the full and effective implementation of 
the Convention by all States Parties (C-8/DEC.16, dated 24 October 2003).

Planned increase in the Article VI inspection programme for 2003 

13.4 
The Council at its Thirty-Fourth Session had noted the Note by the Director-General

AGENDA ITEM EIGHTEEN - Reports of subsidiary bodies 
The Committee of the Whole 
18.1 
The Conference noted that during this session of the Conference no items had been

The General Committee

action. 
The Confidentiality Commission 

18.3

19. 
AGENDA ITEM NINETEEN  Any other business 
Complaint before the International Labour Organisation Administrative 
Tribunal 

19.1 
With reference to this matter, the International Labour Organisation Administrative 
Tribunal (ILOAT) handed down its judgment no. 2232 on 16 July 2003 (see the Note 
by the Chairman of the Conference of the States Parties, C-8/1, dated 24 July 2003) in 
the case brought before it by the former Director-General of the OPCW, 
Mr José Mauricio Bustani. The Conference noted this judgment. 

C-8/7 
page 10 

19.2 
The Conference instructed the OPCWs lawyers to approach Mr Bustanis lawyers to 
arrange a settlement. If agreed, this would constitute a full and final settlement of the 
case. 
19.3 
In the event that the negotiations with Mr Bustani prove unsuccessful, the OPCW 
reserves for itself the right to request the ILOAT to review its judgment no. 2232 with 
respect to the damages awarded. 
19.4 
The Conference requested the Chairperson of the Conference and the 
Director-General to keep the Council and the Conference informed of developments. 
19.5 
The delegations of Brazil, Canada, and the United States of America made statements 
under this agenda item (C-8/NAT.4, C-8/NAT.5 and C-8/NAT.6 respectively, all 
dated 24 October 2003). 
20. 
AGENDA ITEM TWENTY  Date and duration of the next regular session of 
the Conference of the States Parties 
The Conference decided to hold its Ninth Session from 29 November to 
3 December 2004, with the proviso that, should the conference facilities not be 
available during that week, the Ninth Session shall be held from 
6 to 10 December 2004. 

21. 
AGENDA ITEM TWENTY-ONE  Adoption of the report of the Conference of 
the States Parties 
The Conference considered and adopted the report of its Eighth Session. 

22. 
AGENDA ITEM TWENTY-TWO - Closure 
The Chairperson of the Conference closed its Eighth Session at 22:15 on 
24 October 2003.
