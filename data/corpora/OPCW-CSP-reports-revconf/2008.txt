CHAIRPERSON'S REPORT ON THE PROCEEDINGS OF THE CONFERENCE OF THE STATES PARTIES AT ITS THIRTEENTH SESSION 
2  5 DECEMBER 2008 
 
 
The Chairperson of the Thirteenth Session of the Conference of the States Parties (hereinafter the Conference) issues this report under the general powers of the presiding officer (Rule 50 of the Rules of Procedure of the Conference of the States Parties).  As explained in paragraph 22, consensus could not be reached on the adoption of the report of the Conference due to the circumstances described in paragraph 9.7. All other paragraphs contained in this report were approved by consensus. 
 
All decisions adopted by the Conference at its Thirteenth Session, referenced in this report, remain legally valid and binding for all States Parties.  The language of this report also reflects the understandings and interpretations agreed by delegations during the consideration of items on the agenda of the Session.

9.  AGENDA ITEM NINE  Status of implementation of the Convention 


 
Subitem 9(a): Progress report on the implementation of the plan of action regarding the implementation of Article VII obligations 
9.1 The Conference at its Twelfth Session adopted a decision regarding the implementation of Article VII obligations (C-12/DEC.9, dated 9 November 2007), in which it requested the Technical Secretariat (hereinafter the Secretariat), inter alia, to continue to provide its annual report on the status of implementation of Article VII of the Convention and of the decision on follow-up to the plan of action regarding the implementation of Article VII obligations (C-10/DEC.16, dated 11 November 2005) to the Council at its Fifty-Fourth Session.  In its decision, the Conference also requested the Council to submit the progress report to it at its Thirteenth Session, along with its recommendations, as appropriate. 


 
9.2 In its report, the Second Special Session of the Conference of the States Parties to Review the Operation of the Chemical Weapons Convention (Second Review Conference) requested the Conference to continue at its annual sessions to review progress towards the full and effective national implementation of obligations under the Convention and to encourage further progress (subparagraph 9.80(c) of RC-2/4, dated 18 April 2008).  


 
9.3 The Conference noted a report on the status of implementation of Article VII of the Convention as at 15 September 2008 (C-13/DG.6, dated 11 November 2008). 


 
9.4 The Conference approved a decision on the matter (C-13/DEC.7, dated 5 December 2008). 


 
Subitem 9(b): Implementation of the regime governing the handling of confidential information by the Technical Secretariat in 2007 
9.5 The Conference considered and noted the report by the Director-General on the implementation of the regime governing the handling of confidential information by the Secretariat in 2007 (EC-52/DG.5 C-13/DG.1, dated 7 February 2008). 


 
Subitem 9(c): Progress made in meeting revised deadlines for the destruction of chemical weapons 
9.6 Pursuant to a decision by the Conference at its Eleventh Session (paragraph 9.12(b) of C-11/5, dated 8 December 2006), the Conference considered and noted a status report by the Director-General on the progress made by those States Parties that have been granted extensions of deadlines for the destruction of their Category 1 chemical weapons (C-13/DG.7/Rev.1, dated 28 November 2008).  


 
9.7 Several proposals were made to complement the text of paragraph 9.6.  None met the consensus.  Under these circumstances a decision was taken not to include any of these proposals in the text of the report.  After the adoption of paragraph 9.6 a proposal was made that the issue be reopened.  This proposal was not supported by the Conference. 


 
 Subitem 9(d): Implementation of Article X 
9.8 The Conference at its Twelfth Session requested the Council to conduct intensive deliberations to develop measures for emergency assistance to Member States, including with regard to the victims of chemical weapons, as provided in Article X of the Convention, and to report to the Conference at its Thirteenth Session, along with any appropriate recommendations (paragraph 21.8 of C-12/7, dated 9 November 2007). 


 
9.9 The Conference requested the Council to continue further intensive deliberations in line with the above-mentioned mandate given to it by the Conference at its Twelfth Session, and to report to the Conference at its Fourteenth Session.  


 
 Subitem 9(e): Guidelines regarding declaration of import and export data for   Schedule 2 and 3 chemicals 
9.10 The Conference considered and approved a decision on the guidelines regarding declaration of import and export data for Schedule 2 and 3 chemicals (C-13/DEC.4, dated 3 December 2008).

17. AGENDA ITEM SEVENTEEN  Fostering of international cooperation for peaceful purposes in the field of chemical activities 
17.1 At its Twelfth Session, the Conference requested the Council, pursuant to previous decisions, to continue its intensive consultations at regular intervals to develop concrete measures within an agreed framework to ensure the full implementation of Article XI, and to report back to the Conference at its Thirteenth Session for consideration (paragraph 3 of C-12/DEC.10, dated 9 November 2007). 


 
17.2 Recalling the report of the Council at its Fifty-Fourth Session (EC-54/5, dated 17 October 2008), in particular paragraph 5.28, which noted a proposal initiated by the delegation of Cuba to convene a workshop to explore ways and means for the full implementation of Article XI, the Conference recommended the Council to continue and to intensify their deliberations on the elaboration of all the aspects of the workshop, taking into account all the views expressed on it, with a view to the convening of this workshop before the Fourteenth Session. 


 
17.3 The Conference also requested the Council to continue to work, pursuant to previous decisions, to intensify consultations at regular intervals to develop concrete measures within an agreed framework to ensure the full implementation of Article XI, and to report back to the Conference at its Fourteenth Session for consideration.  


18. AGENDA ITEM EIGHTEEN  Ensuring the universality of the Convention 
 
18.1 The Conference noted the annual report by the Director-General on the implementation of the action plan for the universality of the Convention during the period from 3 September 2007 to 25 September 2008 (EC-54/DG.13 C-13/DG.5, dated 29 September 2008 and Add.1, dated 18 November 2008). 

18.2 The Conference noted with satisfaction the substantial progress achieved since the adoption by the Council of the action plan for the universality of the Chemical Weapons Convention (EC-M-23/DEC.3, dated 24 October 2003) and subsequent decisions reaffirming its goals adopted by the Conference. It recalled that at its Twelfth Session the Conference decided to review the results and implementation of that plan at its Fourteenth Session (C-12/DEC.11, dated 9 November 2007).  The Conference commended the Republic of Congo and Guinea-Bissau for having acceded to the Convention since its last Session. 

18.3 The Conference also congratulated the Republic of Lebanon for its decision to accede to the Convention.  Once the Convention enters into force for Lebanon, it will become the 185th State Party. 

18.4 The Conference noted that ten States remain not Party to the Convention, including four signatory States and six non-signatory States, and that amongst the States not Party there are some whose non-adherence continues to be a matter of serious concern.  The Conference reiterated that universality of the Convention is essential to achieve its object and purpose and called upon all remaining States not Party to the Convention (Angola, Bahamas, the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, the Dominican Republic, Egypt, Iraq, Israel, Myanmar, Somalia, and the Syrian Arab Republic) to ratify or accede to it as a matter of urgency and without preconditions in the interests of enhancing their own national security and to affirm their commitment to global peace and security. 

18.5 The Conference noted the annual report by the Director-General on the implementation of the action plan for the universality of the Convention from 3 September 2007 to 25 September 2008 (EC-54/DG.13 C-13/DG.5, dated 29 September 2008), and the Addendum to the said report (EC-54/DG.13/Add.1 C-13/DG.5/Add.1, dated 18 November 2008). 

18.6 The Conference further noted that the Second Review Conference had underlined that the goal of universality shall be pursued as a matter of high priority and called upon the Director-General, the Secretariat, the policy-making organs and all States Parties in a position to do so to intensify their efforts with States not Party with a view to achieving full universality at the earliest possible date.
