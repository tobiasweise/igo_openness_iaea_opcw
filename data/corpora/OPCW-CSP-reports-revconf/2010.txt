9. 	AGENDA ITEM NINE  Status of implementation of the Convention 
The Conference stressed that all issues related to implementation of the Chemical Weapons Convention (hereinafter the Convention) should be dealt with faithfully in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Convention. 
Subitem 9(a): 	Progress report on the implementation of the plan of action regarding the implementation of Article VII obligations 
9.1 	Further to a decision by the Conference at its Fourteenth Session regarding the implementation of Article VII obligations (C-14/DEC.12, dated 4 December 2009), the Conference considered and noted the Note by the Director-General entitled Status of Implementation of Article VII of the Chemical Weapons Convention as at 30 July 2010: Article VII(1)(a) to (c) and Other Obligations (C-15/DG.9, dated 27 October 2010); the Note by the Director-General entitled Status of Implementation of Article VII of the Chemical Weapons Convention as at 30 July 2010: Further Obligations Pursuant to Article VII (C-15/DG.10, dated 27 October 2010); and the Note by the Director-General entitled Progress Regarding Legislative and Regulatory Implementation of Article VII of the Chemical Weapons Convention (Including Assistance Provided) as at 30 July 2010 (C-15/DG.11, dated 27 October 2010). 
9.2 	The Conference stressed that issues in this regard should be dealt with, in accordance with the constitutional processes of States Parties, faithfully in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Convention. 
Subitem 9(b): 	Implementation of the regime governing the handling of confidential information by the Technical Secretariat in 2009 
9.3 	The Conference considered and noted a report by the Director-General on the implementation of the regime governing the handling of confidential information by the Technical Secretariat (hereinafter the Secretariat) in 2009 (EC-59/DG.6 C-15/DG.1, dated 27 January 2010). 
9.4 	The Conference stressed that issues in this regard should be dealt with faithfully in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Convention, including the Annex on the Protection of Confidential Information. 
Subitem 9(c): 	Progress made in meeting revised deadlines for the destruction of chemical weapons 
9.5 	Pursuant to a decision by the Conference at its Eleventh Session (paragraph 9.12(b) of C-11/5, dated 8 December 2006), the Conference considered and noted a report by the Director-General on the progress made by those States Parties that have been granted extensions of deadlines for the destruction of their Category 1 chemical weapons (C-15/DG.13, dated 11 November 2010).  
9.6 	The Conference reaffirmed the obligation of possessor States Parties to destroy their chemical weapons within the extended deadlines, in accordance with the relevant decisions by the Conference at its Eleventh Session, and to that end emphasised the timely commencement of destruction activities at all chemical weapons destruction facilities. 
9.7 	Pursuant to the decisions by the Conference at its Eleventh Session on the final extended deadline, the Conference noted with concern that the final extended deadline of 29 April 2012 may not be fully met.  While noting that significant progress has been made, the Conference also noted that 37.17% of chemical weapons stockpiles remain to be destroyed as of 31 October 2010.  In this regard, the Conference urged all possessor States Parties to take every necessary measure with a view to ensuring their compliance with the final extended destruction deadline.  
9.8 	The Conference stressed in this regard that no action should be undertaken that would undermine the Convention or that would raise questions about the commitment of States Parties, or lead to the rewriting of or reinterpreting of Conventions provisions. 
9.9 	The Conference further stressed that issues in this regard should be dealt with faithfully in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Convention. 
9.10 	In this context, the Conference encouraged the Chairperson of the Executive Council (hereinafter the Council) to continue the informal consultations on how and when to initiate the discussion by the Council on issues related to meeting the final extended deadline for the destruction of chemical weapons as mandated by the Council at its Fifty-Eighth Session. 
Subitem 9(d): 	Extension of deadlines for the destruction of Category 1 chemical weapons stockpiles 
9.11 	The Conference considered and adopted a decision on the extension of the intermediate deadlines for the destruction by the Libyan Arab Jamahiriya of its Category 1 chemical weapons (C-15/DEC.3, dated 30 November 2010). 
9.12 	The Conference stressed that issues in this regard should be dealt with faithfully in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Convention. 
 Subitem 9(e): 	Implementation of Article X 
9.13 	The Conference at its Fourteenth Session requested the Council to continue to deliberate with a view to developing further measures for emergency assistance to Member States, including with regard to the victims of chemical weapons, as provided in Article X of the Convention, and to report back to the Conference at its Fifteenth Session (paragraph 9.11 of C-14/5, dated 4 December 2009). 
9.14 	The Conference at its Twelfth Session requested the Council to conduct intensive deliberations to develop measures for emergency assistance to Member States, including with regard to the victims of chemical weapons, as provided in Article X of the Convention, and to report to the Conference at its Thirteenth Session, along with any appropriate recommendations (paragraph 21.8 of C-12/7, dated 9 November 2007).  
9.15 	The Conference at its Thirteenth Session requested the Council to continue further intensive deliberations in line with the above-mentioned mandate given to it by the Conference at its Twelfth Session, and to report to the Conference at its Fourteenth Session (paragraph 9.9 of C-13/5, dated 5 December 2008).  
9.16 	Pursuant to the recommendation of the Council at its Fifty-Eighth Session (paragraph 
5.27 of EC-58/9, dated 16 October 2009), the Conference at its Fourteenth Session (paragraph 9.11 of C-14/5) requested the Council to continue to deliberate with a view to developing further measures for emergency assistance to Member States, including with regard to the victims of chemical weapons, as provided in Article X of the Convention, and to report back to the Conference at its Fifteenth Session.  
9.17 	The Conference requested the Council to continue its deliberations in this regard, taking into account all proposals from the States Parties relevant to this issue that are being brought forward to the Conference at its Fifteenth Session and/or the Council. In this regard, the Conference noted the proposal of the Islamic Republic of Iran on the establishment of an International Support Network for the Victims of Chemical Weapons (C-15/NAT.2, dated 29 November 2010). The Conference requested the Council to report back to the Conference at its Sixteenth Session, along with any 
appropriate recommendations. 
9.18 	The Conference stressed that issues in this regard should be dealt with faithfully in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Convention. 
Subitem 9(f): 	Revision to the specifications of the approved inspection equipment 
9.19 	The Conference considered the Note by the Secretariat on the proposed update of list of approved inspection equipment with operational requirements and technical specifications and approved the updated list contained therein (C-15/S/2, dated 4 November 2010).

Subitem 16(a): 	OPCW income and expenditure for the financial year to 30 June 2010; and the cash situation and the use of the Working Capital Fund for the financial year to 31 August 2010 
16.1 	The Council at its Sixty-Second Session considered and forwarded to the Conference a report by the Director-General on OPCW income and expenditure for the financial year to 30 June 2010 (EC-62/DG.2 C-15/DG.5, dated 21 July 2010).  The Conference noted this report. 
16.2 	Further to a decision by the Conference at its Fourteenth Session (subparagraph 3(p) of C-14/DEC.8, dated 2 December 2009), the Conference noted a Note by the Director-General on the cash situation and the use of the Working Capital Fund for the financial year to 31 August 2010 (EC-62/DG.6 C-15/DG.7, dated 17 September 2010).   
Subitem 16(b): 	Status of implementation by States Parties of agreed multi-year payment plans to regularise the payment of their outstanding annual contributions 
16.3 	Pursuant to a decision by the Conference at its Eleventh Session (C-11/DEC.5, dated 7 December 2006), the Conference considered and approved the proposal for a multi-year payment plan to regularise the payment of outstanding annual contributions (C-15/DEC.4, dated 1 December 2010). 
16.4 	Pursuant to the same decision, the Conference considered and noted the report on the status of implementation by States Parties of agreed multi-year payment plans (EC-62/DG.5 C-15/DG.6, dated 2 September 2010). 
Subitem 16(c): 	Report of the Office of Internal Oversight for 2009 
16.5 	In accordance with Regulation 12.5 of the OPCW Financial Regulations and Rules, the Council at its Sixty-First Session considered and forwarded to the Conference the annual report of the Office of Internal Oversight for the period from 1 January to 31 December 2009, and the accompanying Note by the Director-General (EC-60/DG.3 C-15/DG.3, dated 23 March 2010). The Conference noted this report. 
Subitem 16(d): 	Transfers of funds between programmes in 2009 
16.6 	In accordance with Regulation 4.5 of the OPCW Financial Regulations and Rules, all transfers between Budget programmes shall be reported to the Conference.  The Conference noted the Note by the Director-General on this matter (EC-59/DG.7 C-15/DG.2, dated 29 January 2010). 
Subitem 16(e): 	Implementation of the International Public Sector Accounting Standards 
16.7 	At its Fourteenth Session, the Conference requested the Secretariat to provide updates on the status of implementation of the International Public Sector Accounting Standards (IPSAS) to the Council at each of its sessions during 2010 and to the Conference at its Fifteenth Session (paragraph 2 of C-14/DEC.5, dated 2 December 2009).  The Conference considered and noted the Note by the Secretariat on the implementation of IPSAS (C-15/S/3, dated 17 November 2010). 
16.8 	
At the same session, the Conference also requested the Director-General to submit proposed amendments to the Financial Regulations of the OPCW to the Council for consideration and subsequent submission, along with the recommendations of the Council, as appropriate, to the Conference at its Fifteenth Session, in accordance with Financial Regulation 16.1 (paragraph 3 of C-14/DEC.5).  The Conference considered and adopted a decision on the amendment of the Financial Regulations of the OPCW (C-15/DEC.5, dated 1 December 2010).

AGENDA ITEM SEVENTEEN  Fostering of international cooperation for peaceful purposes in the field of chemical activities 


17.1 	At its Fourteenth Session the Conference requested the Council, pursuant to previous decisions of the Conference, to intensify consultations at regular intervals to develop concrete measures and recommendations within an agreed framework to ensure the full, effective, and non-discriminatory implementation of Article XI, and report back to the Conference at its Fifteenth Session for consideration (paragraph 1 of C-14/DEC.11, dated 4 December 2009). 
17.2 	The Conference welcomed the holding of a workshop on Article XI of the Convention on 24 and 25 November 2010, and requested the Council to consider the outcome of the aforementioned workshop through the ongoing facilitation process on the full implementation of Article XI of the Convention, in order to develop concrete measures and recommendations within an agreed framework to ensure the full, effective, and non-discriminatory implementation of Article XI of the Convention, as decided in C-14/DEC.11. 
17.3 	
The Conference stressed that issues in this regard should be dealt with faithfully in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Convention.
